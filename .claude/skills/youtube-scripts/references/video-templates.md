## YouTube Tutorial Templates

### Template 1: Match Analysis Breakdown (5-Step Formula)

**Best For:** Post-match content, 5-10 minutes
**Hook Formula:** Show & Teach (quick result + lesson)
**CTA Type:** 75% → next video

```markdown
# How to Analyze [Team A vs Team B] in 5 Minutes ← TITLE WRITTEN FIRST

## Metadata
- Target Length: 7 minutes
- Target Keywords: [Team A] vs [Team B] analysis, [Team A] tactics, football analysis tutorial
- Thumbnail Concept: Split screen - messy spreadsheet vs clean Ball-AI visualization
- Hook Formula: Show & Teach
- CTA Type: 75% → Next Video
- Energy Arc: Empathy → Confidence → Excitement

## Script

### STEP 1: HOOK (0:00-0:30) — Show & Teach Formula
"[Team A] just beat [Team B] [score], and everyone's talking about [key moment/player]. But here's what most analysis is missing—the data that actually explains WHY it happened.

I analyzed this entire match in under 10 minutes using enterprise-grade data, and I found [specific surprising insight]. In the next 5 minutes, I'll show you exactly how I did it and what the data reveals."

[PSYCHOLOGY: Confirms title promise (match analysis) then EXCEEDS it (specific surprising insight + how-to). Creates information gap about the insight.]

### STEP 2: TENSION / OPEN LOOPS (0:30-1:00)
"In this video, you're going to see:"
- "The pressing pattern that completely broke [Team B]'s build-up—and why no one's talking about it"  ← OPEN LOOP 1
- "A single pass network that exposes exactly how [Team A] engineered their chances"  ← OPEN LOOP 2
- "The freeze frame data from the winning goal that shows what really happened"  ← OPEN LOOP 3

[VISUAL: Show "Coming Up" slide with these 3 bullets]
[PSYCHOLOGY: Anticipation Effect—dopamine fires when they know payoff is coming. Each bullet is a partial reveal, not a plain statement.]

### STEP 3: STORY INTRO (1:00-1:45) — Metaphor/Analogy
"Here's the thing about football analysis right now. Most creators are pulling the same FBref stats everyone else uses. It's like every restaurant in town serving the same frozen pizza and calling it artisan. Meanwhile, professional analysts at Sky Sports and The Athletic? They're working with StatsBomb data—metrics like post-shot xG, 360 freeze frames, and pressure sequences. That's the difference between frozen pizza and a proper Italian kitchen. And until recently, that kitchen was locked behind a £40,000/year paywall."

[PSYCHOLOGY: Conceptual Anchoring—frozen pizza vs Italian kitchen makes the abstract data gap instantly visual and memorable.]

### STEP 4: MAIN CONTENT (1:45-5:45) — Problem-First Reveals, Escalating Order

**Point 1: Pressing Pattern [2nd BEST POINT] (1:45-3:00)**
[PATTERN INTERRUPT: Cut to Ball-AI screen recording]
"So here's the question everyone's asking wrong about this match. They're looking at possession stats and shot counts—but that completely misses how [Team A] controlled the game before they even had the ball."
← Problem-first: introduce via the question before revealing the answer
"Let me show you. I'll type: 'What were [Team A]'s pressing patterns in the first 30 minutes?'"
[Wait for generation]
"Look at this pressure map. See how [observation]... This is what PPDA data actually looks like when you have access to it."
← CLOSE LOOP 1 from Step 2
"Now, what's even more interesting is what happened when [Team A] had the ball. And this next visualization might be the most revealing thing I've found..."  ← OPEN NEW LOOP

**Point 2: Pass Network [BEST POINT] (3:00-4:15)**
[PATTERN INTERRUPT: Zoom into visualization, add annotations]
"This next piece of analysis requires zero extra effort to access, but it tells you more about a team's tactical identity than any stat line ever could."
← Problem-first: build up what it reveals before showing it
"'Show me [Team A]'s pass network for the match.'"
[Wait for generation]
"This tells us [insight]. Notice how [player] positioned... This is the kind of data that separates a 200-like thread from a 15K-impression breakdown."
← CLOSE LOOP 2
← Social Proof: reference specific engagement numbers

**Point 3: Freeze Frame [3RD BEST — strong finish] (4:15-5:15)**
[PATTERN INTERRUPT: Picture-in-picture talking head returns]
"For the goal, I want the full context. 'Analyze the buildup to [Team A]'s [Xth] goal with freeze frame data.'"
[Wait for generation]
"This is 360 data—all 22 players positioned at the moment of the pass. You can see exactly [observation]... This is what separates Ball-AI from basic stats. No other free tool gives you this."
← CLOSE LOOP 3
← Peak-End Rule: strong visual payoff at the end

**Summary (5:15-5:45)**
[PATTERN INTERRUPT: Clean summary graphic on screen]
"'Generate a match summary with key metrics.'"
[Wait for generation]
"The numbers confirm what we saw: [key takeaways]."

### STEP 5: CTA (5:45-6:15) — NO WIND-DOWN, 75% → Next Video
"And the pressing pattern we just saw? It's actually part of a bigger tactical trend across [Team A]'s last 5 matches that I break down in my next video. Click right here where I'll show you how their system has evolved—and why their next opponent should be worried."

[NEVER: "Hope that was helpful" / "So those were the 3 things" / any wrap-up language]
[Treat the next video like the next point in THIS video]
```

```markdown
- Energy Arc: Curiosity → Clarity → Confidence → Excitement

[PSYCHOLOGY: Information gap—reveals something important they don't know. Specific promise that exceeds title.]

### STEP 2: TENSION / OPEN LOOPS (0:30-1:00)

- "Why [basic alternative] has been misleading your analysis—and the real example that proves it"  ← OPEN LOOP 1
- "The exact metric that professional analysts use instead, and how to access it without paying £40K"  ← OPEN LOOP 2
- "The most common mistake analysts make with this metric—and I see it on Twitter every single week"  ← OPEN LOOP 3

[VISUAL: "Coming Up" slide with 3 bullets shown on screen while spoken aloud]
```

## Video Length Guidelines

| Content Type | Optimal Length | Purpose |
|--------------|----------------|---------|
| Loom Outreach | 3-4 minutes | Personalized, direct, specific |
| YouTube Tutorial (Quick) | 5-7 minutes | Single concept, match analysis |
| YouTube Tutorial (Deep) | 10-15 minutes | Complex topic, workflow walkthrough |
| YouTube Explainer | 8-12 minutes | Educational, evergreen content |

## Pacing & Retention Tips

- **Loom:** Move quickly, be energetic, respect their time. Give real value (demo) before asking.
- **YouTube Tutorial:** Pattern interrupts every 60-90 seconds. Visual variety is non-negotiable.
- **YouTube Explainer:** Slower pace for comprehension, but still use open loops between sections.
- **All formats:** One core idea per video. If a topic needs multiple ideas, make it a series.
- **Reading level:** Aim for 7th-grade language. Simple isn't dumbing down—it's removing friction.

## CTA Guidelines (75/25 Rule)

**75% of videos → Send to another video (no wind-down):**
"And my next breakdown covers [topic]—click right here where I'll show you..."
Treat the next video as the next point in THIS video. No wrap-up in between.

**25% of videos → Send to website (still no wind-down):**
"Ball-AI is what made this possible—link in the description to get started."
Still use Reciprocity: value first, ask second.

**For ALL videos:**
- One link at the top of every description (above the fold)
- One CTA per video (not multiple)
- Include a small ask too: "Drop a comment with [specific question]" (Commitment ladder)

**Never:**
- "Smash that like button"
- "Hope that was helpful" / "So those were the tips" (wind-down language)
- Aggressive urgency or fake scarcity
- Multiple competing CTAs
